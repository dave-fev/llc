CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION TO LLCs
A Limited Liability Company (LLC) is a business structure that combines the pass-through taxation of a partnership or sole proprietorship with the limited liability of a corporation.

Benefits of an LLC:
- Limited personal liability
- Flexible management structure
- Pass-through taxation
- Less paperwork than corporations

CHAPTER 2: CHOOSING YOUR STATE
Consider factors such as:
- Formation fees
- Annual fees
- Tax requirements
- Privacy laws
- Business-friendly regulations

CHAPTER 3: NAMING YOUR LLC
Your LLC name must:
- Include "LLC" or "Limited Liability Company"
- Be distinguishable from other entities
- Not use restricted words without proper authorization

CHAPTER 4: REGISTERED AGENT
Every LLC must have a registered agent who:
- Has a physical address in the state
- Is available during business hours
- Accepts legal documents on behalf of the LLC

CHAPTER 5: ARTICLES OF ORGANIZATION
Required information typically includes:
- LLC name
- Principal office address
- Registered agent information
- Management structure
- Purpose of the business

CHAPTER 6: OPERATING AGREEMENT
While not always required by law, an Operating Agreement is essential for:
- Defining member roles
- Establishing profit distribution
- Setting management procedures
- Protecting limited liability status

CHAPTER 7: EIN
Apply for an EIN from the IRS:
- Free to obtain
- Required for multi-member LLCs
- Needed for business bank accounts
- Required for hiring employees

CHAPTER 8: BUSINESS LICENSES
Check requirements for:
- Federal licenses (if applicable)
- State licenses
- Local permits
- Professional licenses

CHAPTER 9: STATE-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS
Each state has unique requirements:
- Annual reports
- Franchise taxes
- Publication requirements
- Additional filings

CHAPTER 10: ONGOING COMPLIANCE
Maintain your LLC by:
- Filing annual reports
- Paying required fees
- Maintaining registered agent
- Keeping records updated
- Filing tax returns
